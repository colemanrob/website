Notes on caseworker discretion

Last updated on Oct 11, 2020 1 min read

Caseworker Discretion and the effectiveness of welfare to work programs

  • direct matching to vacancies improves exit
  • program participation leads to worse outcomes including compared to offering no guidance
  • caseworkers, even with information on effectiveness make non-optimal assignments, reducing discretion makes sense

Why do tougher caseworkers increase employment? The role of program assignment as causal mechanism

  • research indicates less accommodating caseworkers are more successful in placing people in employment
  • the results are due less to effective program mix of interventions are more to threat of sanctions

What is the value added by caseworkers

  • caseworker do not efficiently assign clients to interventions based on effectiveness
  • distortions in the decision-process could have a number of reasons;
    • lack appropriate knowledge
    • goals other than efficiency
    • interference from other regulations
